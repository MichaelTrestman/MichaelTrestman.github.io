What are three of your favorite websites? Ask yourself...

Wikipedia

Why is this one of your favorite sites? Wikipedia represents perhaps the largest project in knowledge agglomeration in human history. It is an encyplopedia of staggering scope, that would have awed intellectuals from throughout human history if they could have even conceived of its possibility. We are a priveleged few to live in a time that wikipedia exists. It is a anthropogenic miracle, one of the wonders of the world.

 What area of the site is your eye drawn to when looking at the homepage? Stand back further, what area is your eye drawn to now? Is that area the most important area of the site? Wikipedia actually is sort of unusual in that most visitors don't see the root page. At least, that is my strong suspicion. I'd guess most visitors are directed to a content-page (i.e. an article) by a google search, or by following an internal link from another Wikipedia page. The 'Main Page', http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page follows the format of other pages, highlighting the text content by keeping other things out of the way.

How would you describe the website visually? List 5 adjectives. (i.e. "pretty", "elegant", "simple", "dark", "cluttered", "basic" etc)
  • Functional
  • Minimal
  • Simple
  • Timeless
  • Vast
 What problem does this website solve? What content does it have? What 5 adjectives would you use to describe the content, focus, and purpose of the site? (i.e. "practical", "fun", "whimsical", "silly", "serious" etc) How does that compare to the adjectives you used to describe the site visually?
  • Informative (how could this not be on there?)
  • Grand
  • Ambitious
  • Timeless
  • Vast
Obviously the lists connected.

 How easy is it to find what you are looking for from the homepage? How about from another page? As noted above, a typical user doesn't navigate from the homepage. Wikipedia is pervasively integrated into the larger ecosystem of the web. Many wikipedia content pages are among the top few search hits from google and other engines, and most websites that aspire to be informative have links to Wikipedia. How easy is it to browse through all the content of the site? Even if Wikipedia stopped expanding its content now, it would take many lifetimes, but it is expanding all the time. How do you feel after being on the site for a while? (i.e. "bored", "happy", "anxious or hyper", "like I wasted a lot of time" etc)
  • Satisfied,
  • awed,
  • bemused,
  • amused,
  • puzzled,
  • stimulated,
  • educated,
  • empowered,
  • powerful,
  • most of all: informed.

Does the site sell anything? If so, have you purchased any of it? Why or why not? Wikipedia is a nonprofit foundation run on donations, which is frigging awesome. I have donated, and plan to in the future.
